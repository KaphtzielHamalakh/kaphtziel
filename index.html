Why a studio

One work, many doorways

For years these felt like separate projects — writing, teaching, AI, apps, Japan, meditation, Hasidut, music, pilgrimage. They aren't. Each one is a gateway into the same larger journey: a life of awareness, wisdom, and transformation.

Someone might read a book, listen to the podcast, use an app, attend a workshop, walk a temple path in Japan, and keep learning afterward. Each project opens onto the next.

book→podcast→app→workshop→pilgrimage→practice

The name

Kephitzat ha-derekh — the shortening of the path

In Jewish legend, kephitzat ha-derekh names those moments when a journey that might have taken years suddenly becomes possible — because someone, or something, opens a door. The angel of the leap is קפציאל — Kaphtziel.

Medieval Jewish spell books invoked Kaphtziel to jump the road: to cross in a day what would otherwise take a hundred. The Japanese tradition knows the same magic as shukuchi — shrinking the earth. This studio takes its name, and its work, from that idea: building doorways that shorten the path.

The image at right comes from a seventeenth-century Jewish manuscript of practical Kabbalah — the same drawing that opened the original Kaphtziel blog in 2004. Twenty years later, the angel is still on the door.
